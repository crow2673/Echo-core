## Building a Local AI Assistant on Linux: Recent Progress on Echo

In my ongoing journey to build a local AI assistant, Echo, on a Linux machine, I've made some exciting progress recently. Today, I want to share my latest developments, particularly focusing on the ntfy Bridge and the auto-act cycles that have brought us closer to a fully functional two-way communication system.

### The Challenge: ntfy Bridge

The challenge at hand is to build a two-way phone communication system where Echo can receive and respond to messages from Andrew's phone using the ntfy.sh bridge. This is part one of a two-part challenge, with the second part to be tackled by Echo herself once she tests the system.

#### Logs and Progress

- **Logs/feedback_log.json**: This log file tracks the progress and feedback from Echo.
- **Logs/auto_act.log**: This log file records the actions taken by Echo during the auto-act cycles.
- **Logs/self_coder.log**: This log file captures the self-coding efforts of Echo, including fixes and improvements.
